Candy Dispenser

In a Product Realization course, I undertook a final project to design and create a candy dispenser. The project came with specific design specifications, presenting both a creative and engineering challenge. The key requirements for the candy dispenser were:

    Storage Capacity: Can store up to twenty candies - the "candies" were plastic spheres, about 1mm in diameter​ Single-Serve Dispensing: The mechanism should dispense one candy at a time. Automatic Reset: The dispenser should reset automatically after each use. Rotary Motion: Some form of rotary motion needs to be incorporated Designated Release Spot: The candy dispenser should have a designated spot where the candy is released. Manufacturing Constraints: Components had to be either 3D printed or laser cut.

Design Process

  • Initial Sketches and Research:

    The project began with creating initial sketches of various design concepts. I researched mechanisms that could incorporate rotary motion and settled on a crank and slider mechanism. This setup involved turning a crank to move a slider that would push the candies out of the machine.

  • Rapid Prototyping:

    I developed early prototypes for the key components:

  • Candy Holder: Designed to hold up to 20 pieces of candy.
  • Slider: Responsible for pushing the candy into the dispensing hole, with a spring for automatic resetting.
  • Crank and Slider Mechanism: Translates the rotary motion of the crank into the linear motion of the slider.

  • CAD Modeling:

    I created a basic 3D model of the candy dispenser in CAD. Based on prototype testing, I refined the design by modifying the candy holder to a funnel shape and added a base and candy catcher at the end of the chute.

  • Functional Prototype:

    The next step involved creating a functional prototype, with some parts manufactured from rapid prototyping materials and others from final materials. This allowed me to test and adjust the functionality of the dispenser.

  • 3D Printing and Assembly:

    I modeled the entire assembly in CAD and proceeded to 3D print all the components. Once printed, I assembled the candy dispenser and tested its functionality to ensure it met all design specifications.

  • Documentation:

    For the final documentation, I created an engineering drawing for one of the components and an exploded Bill of Materials (BOM) to detail the assembly process and parts used.
