Telescoping Tube Actuator

During my time with the Stanford Solar Car Project, I was tasked with designing a telescoping tube actuator. This actuator was crucial for optimizing the solar array's position during regular stops in the race. The actuator must enable the solar array to be adjusted to maximize energy absorption by positioning it optimally towards the sun.

  • Project Requirements and Specifications
      • Displacement and Tension:
    • The design needed to accommodate 50mm of displacement and 50N of tension, applied through six Bowden cables.
      • Handheld Operation:
    • The actuator must be operable by hand, with the ability to apply tension using one hand.
      • Weight Constraint:
    • The total weight of the actuator should ideally be less than 250g.
      • Bowden Cable Housing:
    • The diameter of the Bowden cable housing is 6mm.
      • Housing Nesting:
    • Holes to nest the end of the cable housings should be approximately 7-10mm deep.
  • Design Process
      • Design Iterations:
    • I began by iterating through various design ideas using CAD software. Each design iteration was evaluated based on the project's specifications and functionality. Feedback from team members was incorporated to refine and improve the designs. The design process involved several iterations to address any issues and optimize the actuator's performance.
      • Hardware Integration:
    • I imported hardware components from McMaster-Carr into the CAD model to ensure compatibility and functionality. This included selecting appropriate fasteners and fittings to match the design specifications.
      • Final Design:
    • After numerous iterations and refinements, the final design was settled upon. The actuator featured an efficient displacement mechanism and a compact, lightweight design. The CAD model accurately represented the final design, and the hardware components from McMaster-Carr were integrated to ensure the actuator met all requirements.
