Tensile Strength Testing Fixture

During my summer internship at Cruise, I designed and implemented a comprehensive tensile strength testing system for evaluating screw inserts across various materials and configurations. This project involved creating a custom CAD design for the test fixtures, running FEA simulations, and conducting physical tests. Python scripts were developed to analyze and visualize the data, resulting in a robust dataset that provided critical insights into material performance for in-house reference. Learn More

Shot Glass Stand

During my senior year at Stanford, I created a shot glass stand inspired by a collection of shot glasses from around the world. The creation of this stand required several manufacturing processes including milling, brazing, sand casting, turning on a lathe, sanding, using a wood router, and tapping holes. The final product is a sleek and meaningful display for four shot glasses at a time. Learn More

Candy Dispenser

For a class project, I had to create a candy dispenser that had rotary motion, was self-resetting, could store multiple candies, and was loadable and dispensable only using one hand. The manufacturing of this project was limited to 3D printing and laser cutting. To execute this, I used a crank and slider mechanism and 3D printed a simple and functional candy dispenser that I designed in CAD. Learn More

Telescoping Tubes Actuator

For Stanford Solar Car, I created a telescoping tube actuator. This device was necessary for locking and unlocking the solar panels on the car so that they could be adjusted to the most optimal angle during the race to maximize the amount of sun light received. I had to optimize my design to make it light weight and easy to operate for the user. Learn More
